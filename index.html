Narcissistic Personality Disorder

  • Definition
    • Being antisocial, melodramatic in which people often display a character of self-obsession, egotistic, and hubris
  • Possible causes: Unknown
      • It is not known what causes NPD because the cause can likely be complex, but it can be linked to:
    • Mismatches in parent-child relationships with either excessive pampering or excessive criticism
    • Genetics or psychobiology — the connection between the brain and behavior and thinking
  • Symptoms:
    • Having an exaggerated sense of self-importance
    • Expecting to be recognized as superior even without achievements that warrant it
    • Exaggerating your achievements and talents
    • Being preoccupied with fantasies about success, power, brilliance, beauty or the perfect mate
    • Believing that you are superior and can only be understood by or associate with equally special people
    • Requiring constant admiration
    • Having a sense of entitlement
    • Expecting special favors and unquestioning compliance with your expectations
    • Taking advantage of others to get what you want
    • Having an inability or unwillingness to recognize the needs and feelings of others
    • Being envious of others and believing others envy you
    • Behaving in an arrogant or haughty manner
  • Treatment
    • Physcotherapy
    • Hospitalization
    • No Medications


Bipolar Disorder

  • Definition
    • also called manic-depressive disorder - episodes of unpredictable mood swings, ranging from depressive lows to manic, uncontrollable highs.
  • Possible causes
    • consists of the person’s natural state: possibly inherited traits, imbalance in brain chemicals or, stimuli from environment.
    would consist of the person’s natural state: possibly inherited traits, imbalance in brain chemicals or, stimuli from environment.
  • Symptoms:
    • Inflated self-esteem or grandiosity
    • Decreased need for sleep (for example, you feel rested after only three hours of sleep), etc.
    • Depressed mood most of the day, nearly every day, such as feeling sad, empty, hopeless or tearful (in children and teens, depressed mood can appear as irritability)
    • Markedly reduced interest or feeling no pleasure in all — or almost all — activities most of the day, nearly every day
    • Distractibility, No composure
    • Increased goal-directed activity (either socially, at work or school, or sexually) or agitation
    • Doing things that are unusual and that have a high potential for painful consequences — for example, unrestrained buying sprees, sexual indiscretions or foolish business investments
  • Treatment
    • Antidepressants, medical drugs
    • Hospitalization
    • Multiple sessions of therapy

Major Depression

  • Definition
    • Mood disorder that causes constant sadness and loss of interest.
  • Causes
    • Chemical imbalance in the brain and stressful life events

        “I was only thirteen, and they were going to have me psychoanalyzed and all, because I broke all the windows in the garage... I broke all the goddam windows with my fist, just for the hell of it.”

        pg (39)
  • Symptoms:
    • Mood: anxiety, discontent, guilt, hopelessness, inability to feel pleasure, loss of interest, mood swings, or sadness
    • Sleep: odd sleeping habits, insomnia
    • Whole body: excessive hunger, fatigue, loss of appetite, or restlessness, poor appetite
    • Weight: gain or loss
    • Behavior: agitation, excessive crying, irritability, or social isolation
    • Comprehension: lack of concentration, slowness in activity and thought, or thoughts of suicide, repeatedly going over thoughts

        • “I was feeling sort of lousy. Depressed and all. I almost wished I was dead”

        pg (90)
  • Treatment:
    • Therapy
    • Medications

Autism:

  • Definition
    • full term is Autism spectrum disorder
    • disorder that involves the brain development and the social life of a person
    • common in premature births
  • Possible causes:
    • develops from genes and environmental factors which influences the brain
    • deprivation of oxygen during pregnancy
  • Symptoms:
    • having difficulties with social interactions/ communication
    • disengaging from social engagements
    • repetitive behaviors
    • having trouble understanding others/ portraying oneself
    • lack of eye contact
  • Treatment
    • taking prenatal vitamins with folic acid/ consuming lots of folic acid during pregnancy
    • having training sessions/ counseling
